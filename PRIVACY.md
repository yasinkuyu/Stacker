# Privacy Policy for Stacker

**Last updated:** January 8, 2026

At **Insya** ("we," "us," or "our"), we respect your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle data in **Stacker**, our local development environment application.

## 1. Data Collection and Usage

**Stacker is a local-first application.**
We do not collect, store, share, or transmit any of your personal data, source code, database contents, or usage habits to our servers or any third parties.

*   **Local Processing:** All services (PHP, Nginx, Apache, MySQL, etc.) run locally on your machine.
*   **Local Storage:** Your project files, databases, logs, and configuration files are stored strictly on your local hard drive (typically in `~/Library/Application Support/Stacker` on macOS).

## 2. Third-Party Services

Stacker may integrate with third-party tools to enhance your workflow (e.g., deploying to Laravel Forge).
*   **Direct Interaction:** When you use these features, Stacker communicates directly with the third-party service using your own API tokens or credentials.
*   **No Intermediary:** We do not intercept, store, or see these credentials. They are saved locally on your device (often in your system's Keychain) and transmitted directly to the service provider.

## 3. Analytics and Tracking

**No Analytics.**
Stacker does not contain any user tracking, usage analytics, or "telemetry" scripts. We do not know who is using Stacker or how they are using it.

## 4. Crash Reports

By default, crash reports are handled by your operating system (e.g., Apple's built-in crash reporting on macOS). These reports are subject to Apple's Privacy Policy and are only shared with us if you explicitly opt-in to share crash data with developers via your system settings.

## 5. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our application or legal requirements. Since we do not collect user emails, we recommend checking this file on our GitHub repository for updates.
